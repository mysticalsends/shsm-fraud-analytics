DOJ Health Care Fraud Enforcement Actions — 2024

A Data Analysis of False Claims Act Recoveries

Methodology Note

This report is based on DOJ press releases identified as healthcare fraud recoveries in calendar year 2024. We may have missed some cases. Each press release was classified into a single fraud type and a single defendant type. Where multiple types applied — for example, a case involving both a physician and a hospital, or both overbilling and medical necessity — we selected the most prominent. For cases involving multiple U.S. Attorney districts, we chose the first district named in the press release and prioritized individual districts over Main Justice. We believe this provides a detailed look at a single year of DOJ enforcement actions against healthcare companies.

Summary of Findings

In calendar year 2024, the Department of Justice announced 111 healthcare fraud settlements and judgments totaling $2.65 billion in recoveries under the False Claims Act. These cases spanned a wide range of fraud types, defendant categories, and federal districts, reflecting the breadth of the government's health care enforcement program.

Drug Dispensing / Opioids was the top fraud type by dollars recovered, generating $1.14 billion across just 6 cases — driven largely by the McKinsey ($650M), Endo International ($464.9M), and Teva Pharmaceuticals ($450M) settlements. By case count, however, Anti-Kickback / Referral dominated with 39 cases, underscoring the government's sustained focus on improper financial relationships between referral sources and healthcare providers.

Pharmaceutical Manufacturers accounted for the largest share of total recoveries by defendant type at $987.7M across 5 cases, followed by Consulting / Advisory firms at $653.1M (4 cases). By case count, Physician / Practice defendants were named most frequently (29 cases), followed by Clinical Laboratory / Diagnostic Imaging entities (18 cases) and Hospitals / Health Systems (14 cases).

Three districts accounted for nearly 65% of total dollar recoveries: the District of Massachusetts ($740.5M, 10 cases), the Southern District of New York ($495.1M, 5 cases), and the Eastern District of Pennsylvania ($479.7M, 3 cases). Despite this geographic concentration of large-dollar cases, enforcement was geographically broad — 44 different U.S. Attorney districts were represented in the 2024 data.

A small number of large settlements drive a disproportionate share of total recoveries. The top 5 settlements alone — McKinsey ($650M), Endo International ($464.9M), Teva Pharmaceuticals ($450M), Walgreens ($106.8M), and Rite Aid / Elixir ($101M) — account for over $1.77 billion, or approximately 67% of all 2024 recoveries.

Taken together, the 2024 data reflects a Department of Justice that pursues healthcare fraud across a diverse mix of fraud theories and defendant types — from large pharmaceutical kickback schemes to small-dollar physician billing irregularities — while achieving the most dollar impact through a handful of blockbuster settlements each year.
