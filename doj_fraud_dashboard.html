Qui Tam Activity — New Lawsuits Filed & Relator Awards Whistleblower / Relator Program

Tracks two dimensions of the whistleblower program: the annual volume of new qui tam suits filed by relators (bars, left axis) and the total dollar amount paid back to those relators as their statutory share of recovered funds (line, right axis). Relator awards typically range from 15–30% of the recovery.

Qui Tam vs. Government-Initiated Recoveries ($ Billions) Whistleblower Impact

Compares the dollar value of FCA recoveries from whistleblower-initiated qui tam suits against recoveries from government-initiated (non-qui tam) investigations. Qui tam suits have increasingly dominated total recoveries since the 1986 FCA amendments, reflecting the critical role of insider whistleblowers in uncovering fraud that federal investigators would otherwise not detect.

Qui Tam Recovery & Relator Award per Suit Filed Per-Suit Efficiency

Average FCA recovery from qui tam cases (green, left axis) and average relator award paid (purple, right axis) per new qui tam suit filed that year. Because suits typically resolve 3–7 years after filing, these values reflect the output of prior-year filings rather than same-year resolution — read as a macro efficiency signal, not a per-case prediction.
